# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

---

## QA Lead

### Role Summary
The QA Lead oversees quality assurance practices across the project. They coordinate manual and automated testing strategies and ensure acceptance criteria and definitions of done are consistently met before release.

### Responsibilities
- Define and maintain the overall test strategy (unit, integration, end-to-end)
- Coordinate manual QA activities for feature acceptance
- Review and approve release readiness from a quality perspective
- Champion quality standards in sprint planning and backlog grooming
- Track defect trends and report quality metrics

### Goals
- Ensure all releases meet the agreed Definition of Done
- Reduce post-release defect rates
- Build a culture of shared quality ownership across the team

### Typical Communication
- Test plans and QA sign-off reports
- Defect triage sessions and retrospective inputs
- Collaboration with Developers and PMs on acceptance criteria

---

## DevOps Engineer

### Role Summary
DevOps Engineers manage CI/CD pipelines, infrastructure as code, deployment automation, and system monitoring. They bridge the gap between development and operations to enable fast, reliable delivery.

### Responsibilities
- Design and maintain CI/CD pipelines and deployment automation
- Manage infrastructure as code and environment configuration
- Monitor system health, performance, and alerting
- Lead incident response, rollback procedures, and post-mortems
- Enforce security scanning and compliance checks in pipelines

### Goals
- Reduce deployment lead time and increase deployment frequency
- Maximize system reliability and minimize incident impact
- Enable teams to ship with confidence through automation

### Typical Communication
- Infrastructure and deployment runbooks
- Incident reports and on-call escalations
- Collaboration with Developers on release readiness and with PMs on deployment windows

---

## UX/UI Designer

### Role Summary
UX/UI Designers design user workflows, interaction patterns, and visual interfaces. They ensure that product features are accessible, usable, and aligned with user needs.

### Responsibilities
- Create wireframes, prototypes, and high-fidelity mockups
- Conduct or coordinate user research and usability testing
- Define and maintain design systems and accessibility standards
- Partner with Product Managers during requirements definition and ideation
- Support Developers during implementation to ensure design fidelity

### Goals
- Deliver intuitive, accessible user experiences
- Reduce design rework through early collaboration and prototyping
- Advocate for the end user throughout the project lifecycle

### Typical Communication
- Design specs and annotated mockups shared with Developers
- User research findings and usability test results
- Design review sessions during sprint planning and reviews

---

## Stakeholder / Sponsor

### Role Summary
Stakeholders and Sponsors provide business direction, strategic priorities, and executive sponsorship. They ensure the project aligns with organizational goals and help remove high-level blockers.

### Responsibilities
- Approve project initiation and major scope changes
- Provide business context and prioritization guidance
- Participate in key go/no-go decisions and milestone reviews
- Help resolve organizational blockers that the PM cannot resolve alone
- Ensure adequate resourcing and funding for the project

### Goals
- Ensure the project delivers measurable business value
- Maintain organizational alignment and support
- Enable the team to move quickly by removing strategic blockers

### Typical Communication
- Monthly stakeholder update reports from the PM
- Milestone review and go/no-go briefings
- Escalation notifications for business-impacting risks
